

code: GEOB 102
name: Our Changing Environment: Climate and Ecosystems
cred: 3
desc: Energy and water in the Earth-Atmosphere system, global climates and climate change, ecosystem properties and processes, human impacts. Please consult the Faculty of Science Credit Exclusion Lists: www.students.ubc.ca/calendar/index.cfm?tree=12,215,410,414. [3-2*-0]

code: GEOB 103
name: Our Changing Environment: Water and Landscapes
cred: 3
desc: Physical processes acting at the Earth's surface; water cycle; landforms; human impacts. Please consult the Faculty of Science Credit Exclusion Lists: www.students.ubc.ca/calendar/index.cfm?tree=12,215,410,414. [3-2*-0]

code: GEOB 200
name: Atmospheric Environments
cred: 3
desc: Physical principles underlying weather and climates. Thermal, moisture and wind climates at scales from valleys to the globe. Daily weather, air pollution, global change. Credit will be given for one only of GEOB 200, 204, GEOG 200, 204, AGRO 244. [3-2*-0]
preq: GEOG 102 or GEOB 102 or first-year science

code: GEOB 204
name: Introduction to Biometeorology
cred: 3
desc: Basic principles and processes of climatology. Energy and water balance concepts. Weather systems and climate change, microclimate of soils, crops, forests, and animals. Credit will be granted for only one of GEOB 200, 204, APBI 244. [3-2-0]

code: GEOB 206
name: Geomorphic Processes and Hazards
cred: 3
desc: Earth surface processes shaping the landscape; identification of hazards and hazardous areas associated with these processes; introduction to techniques for measurement and analysis at various spatial and time scales; field trips are required. [3-2*-0]
preq: GEOB 103 or EOSC 110 or PHYS 101 or PHYS 107 or PHYS 153 or SCIE 001

code: GEOB 207
name: Introduction to Biogeography
cred: 3
desc: Geographical ecology emphasizing plant distributions, abiotic-biotic interactions, effects of disturbance, succession, and human impacts across scales. Labs and field trips examine a local site. [3-2*-0]
preq: (GEOG 102 or GEOB 102) and (GEOG 103 or GEOB 103)

code: GEOB 270
name: Geographic Information Science
cred: 3
desc: Computer-based graphical methods of data input and analysis. Emphasis on data visualization techniques such as cartographic modelling and exploratory data analysis. Preclusion: Not available to those who completed GEOG 370 before September 2005. [2-2-0]

code: GEOB 300
name: Microscale Weather and Climate
cred: 3
desc: Meteorology and climatology at the micro-, local, and meso-scales. Interactions between land surfaces and atmosphere. Basics of atmospheric turbulence and transfer processes. Microclimates on scales of a leaf to those of a large valley. [3-0-0]
preq: GEOB 200 or GEOB 204 or ATSC 201

code: GEOB 304
name: Synoptic Meteorology and Climatology
cred: 3
desc: Analysis of meteorological fields, diagnostic analysis of synoptic weather systems, applications of synoptic meteorology. Requires participation in weekly weather map discussion. [2-2-0]
preq: GEOG 200 or GEOB 200 or ATSC 201

code: GEOB 305
name: Introduction to Hydrology
cred: 3
desc: Principles of hydrology at site, watershed, and larger regional scales. Introduction to techniques of measurement and analysis. Emphasizes surface water hydrology of western North America. [3-2*-0]
preq: GEOB 200 or GEOB 204 or APBI 244 or ATSC 201

code: GEOB 307
name: Biogeography and Global Change
cred: 3
desc: Biogeographic concepts in understanding responses in ecosystems to environmental change at global, regional, and local scales. Conservation issues such as the loss of biodiversity and endangered species. [3-2*-0]
preq: BIOL 230 or GEOB 207 or FRST 202

code: GEOB 308
name: Quaternary and Applied Geomorphology
cred: 3
desc: Quaternary landscape development emphasizing the history of glaciation with special reference to western North America; applications of geomorphology in resource development and land management; interpretation of quaternary materials. Weekend field trips required. [3-2*-0]
preq: EOSC 320 or EOSC 330 or GEOB 206

code: GEOB 309
name: Geographical Sciences Field Course
cred: 3
desc: Field sampling, instrumentation, surveying, mapping techniques, and data analysis; mandatory multi-day field camp in April or May. Special fees and liability insurance are required. 
preq: (BIOL 230 or GEOB 207) and (GEOB 200 or GEOB 204)
creq: GEOB 206 or EOSC 330

code: GEOB 370
name: Advanced Geographic Information Science
cred: 3
desc: Theoretical and practical aspects of Geographic Information Systems, including cartographic modeling, digital terrain models, management issues, and spatial interpolation. [2-2-0]
preq: GEOB 270. Or third-year standing in ENSC or GEOB specializations or with some introductory knowledge of GIS

code: GEOB 372
name: Cartography
cred: 3
desc: Cartographic methods: development of cartography; projections; data ordering, compilation and symbolization; cartographic design, map reproduction. [2-2-0]
preq: 6 credits from GEOB 102 GEOB 103 GEOB 200 GEOB 204 GEOB 207

code: GEOB 373
name: Introductory Remote Sensing
cred: 3
desc: Aerial photography; measurement from aerial photographs; photo-interpretation in geographic analysis; remote sensing of the earth's surface and atmosphere. [3-2*-0]
preq: GEOB 270. Or third-year standing in ENSC or GEOB specializations

code: GEOB 400
name: Global Change Science
cred: 3
desc: Processes controlling the planetary cycles of elements like carbon, nitrogen and phosphorus, the human perturbation of these biogeochemical cycles, and the consequences for the atmosphere, terrestrial ecosystems, and aquatic ecosystems. [3-0-1]
preq: (BIOL 121 and CHEM 111) or (BIOL 121 and CHEM 121) or SCIE 001. Fourth year standing is also required

code: GEOB 401
name: Urban Meteorology
cred: 3
desc: The impact of urbanization upon atmospheric processes and climates. The energy and water balances of cities. Meteorological effects (urban heat island, precipitation modification, etc.) and their significance. Models of the urban atmosphere. [3-0-0]
preq: GEOG 204 or GEOB 204 or GEOG 300 or GEOB 300 or AGRO 244 or APBI 244

code: GEOB 402
name: Air Pollution Meteorology
cred: 3
desc: The nature of atmospheric pollutants. The ability of the atmosphere to disperse, transform, and remove pollutants. Air pollution dispersion models. Air quality monitoring, criteria, and standards. [3-0-0]
preq: GEOB 200 or GEOB 300

code: GEOB 403
name: Catchment Hydrology
cred: 3
desc: Hydrometeorology; runoff processes; streamwater chemistry in relation to runoff processes; effects of climate variations and land cover change on streamflow regimes; models of catchment hydrology. [3-0-0]
preq: (GEOB 200 or GEOB 204) and (GEOB 305 or FRST 385) and (STAT 200 or FRST 231 or BIOL 300)

code: GEOB 405
name: Fluvial Geomorphology
cred: 3
desc: Introduction to open channel flow and sediment transport. River morphology and channel types. Palaeohydrology. The development of channel networks. [2-3-0]
preq: EOSC 320 or EOSC 330 or GEOB 206

code: GEOB 406
name: Watershed Geomorphology
cred: 3
desc: Hillslope and stream channel processes and their rates of operation. Analysis and modeling geomorphic processes and associated hazards at the watershed scale. Applications in geoscience and engineering. [2-2-0]
preq: GEOB 206 or EOSC 330

code: GEOB 407
name: Vegetation Dynamics: Disturbance, Climate and Human Impacts
cred: 3
desc: Investigation of vegetation dynamics integrating theory and research to address contemporary issues of global environmental change. Students will conduct fieldwork and learn to analyze data and interpret results. [2-3-0]
preq: BIOL 230 or GEOB 207 or FRST 201

code: GEOB 408
name: Snow and Ice Processes
cred: 3
desc: Formation of snow and ice masses and their evolution including snowpacks, glaciers, and ice sheets. Relationships between snow, ice, and climate including glacier dynamics and avalanche forecasting. Weekend field trip. [3-0-0]
preq: (PHYS 101 or PHYS 107 or PHYS 153 or SCIE 001) and (GEOB 305 or GEOB 308)

code: GEOB 409
name: Self-Directed Field Studies in Physical Geography
cred: 3
desc: Advanced research design, field sampling methods, instrumentation, surveying, mapping techniques, data analysis and management; self-directed fieldwork; 1 lecture per week during term and a multi-day field camp in April or May. [2-0-0]
preq: GEOB 309. Third-year standing in Geographical Biogeosciences Major

code: GEOB 448
name: Directed Studies in Geographical Sciences
cred: 3, 4
desc: For fourth-year students in Geography to permit investigation of a topic to be agreed upon by a member of the faculty and the student. Permission of the department head and of a supervisory faculty member is required. Credit will be given for only one of GEOB 448 and GEOG 448. 

code: GEOB 449
name: Undergraduate Thesis
cred: 3, 6
desc: Original research on an approved topic, developed under the guidance of a faculty member. Open to 4th year students in Geographical Biogeosciences with appropriate background courses related to the research topic, and permission of the directing faculty member. The 3-credit option is only available to students who complete GEOB 449 in tandem with GEOB 409.

code: GEOB 490

cred: 3

Credit will not be granted for both GEOG 442 and GEOB 490.
