

code: FNEL 101

code: FNEL 380
name: Technologies for Endangered Language Documentation and Revitalization
cred: 3
desc: Digital tools for endangered language documentation, conservation, and revitalization. Overview of best practices, introduction to community engagement and capacity-building, protocols and ethics, project design, cultural context, orthographies, use of audio, video and still photography, data management, archiving and web publishing. 

code: FNEL 381
name: Biocultural Diversity: Language, Community, and Environment
cred: 3
desc: Critical exploration of the links between linguistic, biological and cultural diversity; including connections with linguistic relativity, linguistic identity, language and place; and strategies for collective, interdisciplinary action to promote and support the protection of languages, cultures, and the environment. 

code: FNEL 382
name: Lexicography for Endangered Languages
cred: 3
desc: Foundational concepts in the discipline of compiling, editing, managing and hosting dictionaries, also known as lexicography. Special focus on the technical and ethical considerations for community-based lexicography projects for endangered and BC First Nations languages. 

code: FNEL 389
name: Collaborative Heritage Research in First Nations Languages: Practicum & Workshop
cred: 3
desc: Hands-on experience planning and producing a Breath of Life Language Reclamation Workshop for endangered BC First Nations languages, during which community-based researchers access and interpret relevant heritage resources at UBC and other local institutions for the revitalization of their languages. 
creq: FNEL 281 and FNEL 282. Corequisites can be taken either alongside and prior to FNEL 389

code: FNEL 480
name: Endangered Language Documentation and Revitalization
cred: 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11
desc: Critical study of the historical, social, cultural, political, and economic factors impacting on language loss, retention, and revival. code: FNEL 481
name: Heritage Resources in Endangered First Nations Language Revitalization
cred: 3
desc: Examines the complementary and intersecting roles of libraries, archives, and museums in collections acquisition, development, curation, preservation, and access traditions pertaining to First Nations languages, to explore how each can contribute to endangered language and cultural heritage sustainability. 
creq: FNEL 281 and FNEL 282. Corequisites can be taken either alongside and prior to FNEL 481

code: FNEL 482
name: Applied Research in Endangered Language Reclamation
cred: 3
desc: Application of research skills and methodologies in the design and implementation of a research project relevant to endangered language conservation and/or revitalization. Project co-constructed in collaboration with a First Nations community, guided by community-based ethical protocols. FNEL 481 recommended. 
creq: FNEL 281 and FNEL 282 and FNEL 389. Corequisites can be taken either alongside and prior to FNEL 482
